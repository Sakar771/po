About

I am a current M.Sc. Student in Remote Sensing and Geographic Information Systems at the Asian Institute of Technology (AIT), Thailand. I did B.Sc. in Forestry from Institute of Forestry, Tribhuvan University, Nepal. Living in the vicinity of the buffer zone of Chitwan National Park, I have an unparalleled care for nature and its significance in our life. I am Geography and Environment proponent, Cartographer, Earth Oberservant, RS/GIS Practicioner and Geo-spatial data analyst. I have a desire to change the world and tackle climate change with GIScience.

My research interest lies in spatio-temporal analysis of environment change patterns such as forest degradation, wildfire, insects attack, invasive species outbreak, river, etc and land cover change detection integrating human-nature relationships. During my study period, I gained plenty of experience related to forest management including field works, data collection and data analysis both individually and with a team. My expertise lies in environment conservation combined with Geo-informatics. Most of the time I spend my time on innovating with cartography, online RS/GIS training and research activities. From programming languages such as R, JavaScript API and Python to GIS software as ArcGIS, ArcGIS Pro and QGIS, they are my daily-drivers: both quantitative and qualitative analysis. Lately, I have started to delve more into Python for GIS and blend maps with AutoCAD and Adobe products.
